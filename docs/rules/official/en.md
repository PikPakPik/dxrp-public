&nbsp;&nbsp;<summary> ğŸ”¶Building </summary>

  - Houses require a minimum of 1 entrance. You can block off doors that you don't use. <br>
  - Bases must always be accessible without the need to place props or entities. <br>
  - Builds should not take an unnecessary amount of public space. If you build in public areas, be reasonable.
  - If you can shoot at someone, they must be able to see you and be able to shoot back at you. <br>
  - You may not force players to crouch or jump at any any point in your base, even to fight back. <br>
  - Bases cannot be built in a way that will cause players to get stuck permanently in any part of it. <br>
  - Bases may not have mazes built within to artificially extend the raid duration. <br>
  - Bases may not purposefully impede player movement (Such as placing props too close to each other to slow down players).
  - Reaching a base's entrance should not rely on luck / a glitch / an exploit. <br>
  - The entrance of a base must be reasonably easy to find.
    - It should be big enough to be noticeable, and stand out in some way (color, different prop from surroundings, etc).
  - You cannot block off map features (weed drop off, trash cans, ATMs). They must always be clearly accessible.
    - You must always be able to walk over the entire weed drop off. No props can be placed in a way that would affect player movement over it.
  - Only purchase doors to buildings and rooms you will actually use.
  - If your base can harm other players, make sure that it has reasonable counterplay that does not rely on luck.
  - All bases should be raidable with a crow bar, you cannot make players rely on finding a code.

&nbsp;&nbsp;<summary> ğŸ”¶Fading Doors </summary>
  - A maximum of 2 fading doors can be used as doors per base (This includes any door used to reach valuables or any part of the base). <br>
    -You cannot hide valuables behind multiple fading doors, even if individually they would only require a raider to break 2 fading doors. <br>
  - Fading doors used for other purposes do not count towards that limit. These fading doors cannot be giving an unfair advantage. <br>
    -Other purposes can be an emergency exit (a door that can only be used from one direction), peep holes (fading door that can be toggled to shoot through), etc.
    -If a door can be broken through from the outside, it is not an emergency exit, and counts as a normal door.
  - Always leave enough space between 2 doors (both fading and normal) to be able to comfortably stand. <br>
  - Fading doors must be reasonably easy to see and distinguish from walls.
